invisible cities

This is a digital exploration of Italo Calvino's Invisible Cities, a novel that describes fantastical cities through the conversations between Marco Polo and Kublai Khan. It's one of my favorite texts of all time and I keep coming back to it and trying to recommend others to read it, or just send quotes of it. There's no good online compilation of the chapters that I found easily explorable, so I thought it'd be nice to have a link to send.

Each city represents different themes—memory, desire, signs, thin cities, trading cities, cities and eyes, cities and names, cities and the dead, cities and the sky, continuous cities, and hidden cities.

A lot of the art and images come from various other people on the internet, such as Pooja Tarun Patel and Karina Puente and Gary Garvin and Calvino's cities.

about the text

Invisible Cities was first published in 1972. Calvino's work explores themes of urban planning, human experience, and the relationship between reality and imagination. The cities described are both physical places and metaphors for human conditions and desires. From Wikipedia: Invisible Cities deconstructs an archetypal example of the travel literature genre, The Travels of Marco Polo, which depicts the eponymous Venetian merchant's journey across Asia and in Yuan China (Mongol Empire).

You should check out Calvino's other works: If on a winter's night a traveler and a short story collection Here Comes the Raven, amongst others.
